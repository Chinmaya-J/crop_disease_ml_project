Self-Reflective and Continually-Learning AI Assistant for Crop Disease Diagnosis

Phase II – Baseline Vision Model Implementation

This semester focuses on implementing and evaluating the baseline vision model component of the proposed self-reflective continual AI system for crop disease diagnosis.

Problem Statement

Deep learning models achieve high accuracy in controlled laboratory datasets such as PlantVillage. However, real-world deployment suffers due to environmental variations, new disease patterns, and lack of adaptability. As the foundational step of the proposed adaptive AI assistant, this semester focuses on implementing a robust baseline CNN model for crop disease classification.

Literature Review

Existing research demonstrates high performance of CNN-based models such as ResNet and MobileNet for plant disease detection. However, most systems remain static and lack adaptability or self-reflection mechanisms. This project builds upon established CNN approaches as a baseline before integrating reflective and continual learning components.

Research Gap

While prior works achieve high accuracy, they do not incorporate adaptive or reflective mechanisms. Before implementing reflection and continual learning, it is necessary to establish a strong and reliable baseline vision model. This semester addresses that foundational requirement.

System Methodology

Dataset & Preprocessing

Dataset: PlantVillage ~54,000 leaf images across multiple crop diseases.
80/20 train-validation split.
Images resized to 224x224 and normalized.

Model & Training Strategy

Pretrained MobileNetV3 (ImageNet weights) Frozen backbone for transfer learning
Custom fully connected layer for classification
Loss: CrossEntropyLoss
Optimizer: Adam
Epochs: 3

Results & Analysis

Accuracy / Performance 92.3%

The MobileNetV3-based transfer learning model achieved 92.3% validation accuracy after 3 epochs. Most misclassifications occurred between visually similar disease categories. The results validate the feasibility of the baseline vision module and establish a foundation for integrating reflection and continual learning in future phases.

Next Phase: Integration of Grad-CAM explainability and confidence-based reflection module.
